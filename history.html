History of Prizren

Prizren is one of the oldest cities in Kosovo and has a rich history dating back many centuries over 2,000 years! Illyrian tribes originally inhabited the area, and later it became part of the Roman Empire. The city has been influenced by various empires and cultures over the centuries, including the Byzantine, Serbian, and Ottoman Empires.

(Léon, 1913)

During the 15th century, Prizren became an important city for the Ottoman empire. Many of the city's famous mosques, buildings and bridges were constructed during this period.

(Nova iskra, 1899)

Today Prizren remains an important cultural and historical centre for Albanians, Turks and Serbs in Kosovo. League of Prizren was established in 1878 to protect the rights of Albanians in the Ottoman Empire. Serbs took control of the city during the Balkan Wars in 1912, and it was later incorporated into the Kingdom of Serbia. After World War II, Prizren became part of the Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia. The city has seen significant changes in its demographics and cultural landscape over the years, but it remains a vibrant and diverse community. Turks whom are modern day Ottomans have always had a strong presence in the city and are one of the three official languages in the municipality.
